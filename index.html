Who You Are

Two missions. One powerful
untold story.

NewView Oklahoma serves blind and low-vision individuals through two interconnected missions: a rehabilitation clinic providing mobility and occupational therapy, and an enterprise division employing approximately 90 blind and low-vision workers manufacturing safety kits, hygiene kits, school supply kits, and fire hoses for clients including the U.S. Forestry Service. With recent federal contract losses reducing revenue, NewView is diversifying into commercial markets — and your story has never been more important to tell.

The Opportunity

The stories are there.
The system isn't.

We looked at your social presence, talked with your team, and found something we see often: an organization doing life-changing work with almost no system for telling that story. Here's what stood out.

01
Powerful Stories, Almost No One Hearing Them
NewView has roughly 2,000 social media followers and minimal engagement on most posts. But every time an employee spotlight or patient testimonial goes up, it outperforms everything else. The proof of concept is already in your analytics — your best content is the one you rarely make time to create.
"When I do publish a testimonial on social media, we get a ton of engagement on it."
02
Three Platforms, No Playbook
Facebook and LinkedIn get occasional traction. Instagram is flat. There's no content calendar, no templates, no posting framework for your new marketing coordinator to follow. Ideas live in Kim's head — and Kim is working 60 hours a week. That's not sustainable, and it means your best marketing ideas never make it to execution.
"I just don't have the capacity. And that's why everyone's saying — just use AI to do all this stuff."
03
A Dual Mission the World Doesn't Know About
You run both a rehabilitation clinic for blind and low-vision individuals AND an enterprise division employing approximately 90 workers manufacturing fire hoses, safety kits, and school supplies. That's an extraordinary story — the kind that drives engagement, earns media coverage, and inspires donors. Almost nobody outside your immediate circle knows it exists.
"We get more engagement on employee posts or when we focus on a person that's one of our patients or clients."
Our Approach

People connect with people.
Not logos.

Your highest-performing content already proves the thesis: when you tell human stories — employee spotlights, patient journeys, the daily reality of your mission — people engage. Everything else underperforms. We don't need to reinvent your marketing. We need to build a system around what already works — and give your team the playbook, the calendar, and the coaching to sustain it without burning out.
